About Me

I am currently an NSF Graduate Fellow in the University of Wisconsin-Madison Physics Department. I'm advised by Dr. Ellen Zweibel of the Physics and Astronomy departments, and my research in plasma astrophysics sits at the intersection of these disciplines. More specifically, I explore, through both analytic studies and simulations, how galactic winds expel mass from galaxies and subsequently affect star formation; how energetic particles called cosmic rays play an integral role in launching galactic outflows; and how cosmic rays are accelerated at astrophyiscal shocks, including at galactic wind termination shocks.
